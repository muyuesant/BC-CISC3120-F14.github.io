About CodeLab

By now, you've hopefully tried CodeLab. The idea behind CodeLab is that it's a "safe" place to practice small bits of programming -- for example, getting used to the differences between Java and C++ syntax.

Don't be afraid to play around, to try lots of things that end up not working. CodeLab does keep a history of your attempts, but that's to help both you and me get a handle on what you're not understanding. If you're struggling with a CodeLab exercise, you can email me and ask for help on problem #12345, and I can log in, see what you've tried, and give you advice.

For grading purposes, my main concern is simply the number of exercises you've eventually gotten corrent; I don't care about how many attempts you've made. You'll get a little bit of credit, too, for exercises where you've made a valiant attempt but couldn't quite get it.

You'll either get email from me, or see a posting on the calendar page, when there are new CodeLab exercises assigned; you should generally assume that a new batch will be assigned very shortly after one has become due.
